## 🎯 My Mission

> *I don't write code to fill a screen with syntax. I write code to close the distance between a student in a small town and the opportunity they didn't know existed.*

Growing up in Pakistan, I watched brilliant people around me — cousins, classmates, neighbors — stall out not because they lacked talent, but because they lacked **access**: to guidance, to information about scholarships, to someone who could simply tell them *what to do next*. That gap is not a talent problem. It's a design problem. And design problems can be solved.

That belief is why I'm building **Future With Farhan (FWF)** — an EdTech platform that puts exam prep, scholarship information, university guidance, career direction, and AI mentoring into the hands of every Pakistani student, regardless of what city they were born in or what school they can afford.

I'm not interested in building things that are merely impressive. I'm interested in building things that **matter to someone at 11pm when they don't know what to do with their future.**

## 🔥 Why I Code

Because code is the closest thing I've found to a lever long enough to move something real. A single well-built app doesn't just sit on a phone — it sits in someone's pocket at the exact moment they need it: choosing a university, prepping for an exam, figuring out if a scholarship deadline is still open.

I don't chase trends. I chase **leverage** — the smallest amount of code that creates the largest amount of good.

## 🧠 Developer Philosophy

> **Working software for one real user beats a perfect architecture for zero users.**

I'd rather ship an app that helps one student tonight than spend three more weeks polishing an idea nobody has touched yet. I fix bugs by reading errors, not by guessing. I choose boring, stable technology over exciting, fragile technology when real people are depending on the result. And I believe the best documentation is code that explains itself, followed closely by a README that respects the reader's time.
